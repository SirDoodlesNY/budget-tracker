📚 Help & Information

🎯 Understanding Budget Categories

Each category breaks down your budget into three parts: non-bill spending you want to allow, recurring bills you know are coming, and what you've actually spent.

💰 Budget Non-Bill Limit

Non-bill spending you allow for this category (on top of your bills). For example, $100 for gas and car expenses.

📋 Planned Bills

Recurring bills you've set up that repeat monthly. For example, parking ($65/month).

📊 Total Budget

Non-Bill Limit + Planned Bills. Your complete spending limit for the category.

💸 Spent

Actual money spent: paid bills + additional expenses you've entered.

✓ Remaining

Total Budget minus Spent. Green when on budget, red when over.

Example: Transportation/Gas Category
• Budget Non-Bill Limit: $100 (gas and car expenses)
• Planned Bills: $65 (parking subscription)
• Total Budget: $165
• Spent: $150 (parking paid + $85 in gas)
• Remaining: $15 (still have room this month)

📊 Budget Status Bar

Each category displays a color-coded status bar showing your spending progress:

  • 🟢 Green bar = On budget (spending is within your limit)
  • 🔴 Red bar = Over budget (you've exceeded your limit)
  • ⚫ Gray bar = No budget set for this category

The bar fills based on how much of your total budget you've spent. When you overspend, the bar turns red and shows a warning.

🚀 Getting Started

Step 1: Create Budget Categories

Go to "Budget Planning" tab and set up your spending categories (e.g., Groceries, Gas, Entertainment). Set a "Budget Non-Bill Limit" for non-bill spending in each.

Step 2: Add Recurring Bills

For each category with regular bills (like utilities, subscriptions, car payments), add them as "Recurring Bills". Set them as "Fixed day" (e.g., 10th of month) or "Relative day" (e.g., First Monday).

Step 3: Log Transactions

On the "Dashboard", click "+ Add Transaction" to record your spending. Choose "Bill Payment" when paying a bill so it counts towards that bill's budget.

Step 4: Monitor Progress

Watch your Budget Status bars turn green or red as you spend. Use "Collapse All" to get a quick overview, then expand categories you want to review in detail.

Step 5: Review History

Check "Transaction History" to see all your spending, filtered by month or category. Bill payments show the bill name for easy reference.

📊 Dashboard Tab

Your quick overview of this month's finances at a glance:

  • Total Income (This Month): All money coming in this month
  • Paid Bills (This Month): Bills you've already paid this month
  • Unpaid Bills (This Month): Bills still due this month
  • Non-Bill Budget (This Month): Total of what you've budgeted for non-bill spending (groceries, gas, etc.)
  • Non-Bill Spending (This Month): How much you've actually spent on non-bill items
  • Money Left Over (This Month): Income minus bills minus actual spending
  • Upcoming Bills: Bills due soon this month, sorted by date (click ▼/▶ to collapse/expand)

📈 Budget Planning Tab

Manage your budget categories and recurring bills:

  • Collapse All / Expand All: Quickly show or hide all category details
  • Budget Categories: View spending progress with color-coded status bars
  • Edit Category: Change the "Budget Non-Bill Limit" and rename the category
  • Delete Category: Remove a category (all linked transactions will be removed)
  • Recurring Bills: Manage bills that happen regularly each month
  • Export Data: Download your data as a JSON file for backup
  • Import Data: Restore data from a previously exported file

📜 Transaction History Tab

View and manage all your transactions:

  • Filter by Month: View transactions from any month
  • Filter by Category: See spending in specific categories
  • Reset Filters: Clear filters to see all transactions
  • Bill Payments: Show the bill name for easy tracking (e.g., "Car Insurance - Geico")
  • 🟢 Green = Income: Money coming in
  • 🔴 Red = Expense or Bill: Money going out

💡 Tips & Tricks

  • Dashboard Overview: Check the dashboard daily to see your Paid Bills, Unpaid Bills, and Money Left Over—everything you need to know at a glance
  • Collapse for Quick View: Use "Collapse All" on the Budget Planning tab to hide all category details and see just the budget status bars
  • Collapse Upcoming Bills: Click the ▼ button next to "Upcoming Bills" on the dashboard to hide the list when you don't need it
  • Bills Sorted by Date: Upcoming bills are automatically sorted earliest to latest, so you know which ones are due soonest
  • Delete Category Warning: When deleting a category, you'll see a clear warning that all transaction history for that category will be permanently removed
  • Bill Payment Type: Use "Bill Payment" type when paying bills so they're tracked against your planned bills and show the bill name in history
  • Add Notes: When entering transactions, add notes to remember what purchases were for
  • Monthly Reset: Bills automatically reset each month, so you start fresh tracking payments
  • Regular Backups: Look for the red "!" badge in the header. Click it to be reminded about cloud backups
  • Auto-Backups: The app automatically saves daily backups. You can restore from any previous day if needed
  • Dark Mode: Click the sun/moon icon in the header to toggle dark mode—the app works great in both!
  • Stay Updated: When new versions are available, just download and open the new HTML file. All your data stays!

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What's the difference between "Budget Non-Bill Limit" and "Planned Bills"?

A: The Non-Bill Limit is non-bill spending you want to allow (like $100 for gas). Planned Bills are recurring expenses you know about (like $65 parking). Together they make your Total Budget.

Q: What's the difference between "Paid Bills" and "Unpaid Bills" on the dashboard?

A: "Paid Bills" shows bills you've already paid this month using "Bill Payment" transactions. "Unpaid Bills" shows the total amount of recurring bills that are still due. This helps you see what's done and what's still coming.

Q: What's the difference between "Non-Bill Budget" and "Non-Bill Spending"?

A: "Non-Bill Budget" is the total of what you've budgeted for (like $100 for groceries, $80 for gas, etc.). "Non-Bill Spending" is how much you've actually spent. If you budget $100 for groceries but only spend $75, your Non-Bill Spending shows $75.

Q: How do I pay a recurring bill?

A: Click "+ Add Transaction", select "Bill Payment" as the type, then choose which bill to pay from the dropdown. Enter the amount (it can differ from the planned amount) and submit. The bill will be marked as paid for this month.

Q: Can a category have multiple recurring bills?

A: Yes! For example, the "Transportation" category could have both a parking bill and a car payment. The Planned Bills total will include both, and you'll pay them separately when due.

Q: What happens to my data if I clear my browser cache?

A: Your data is stored separately from browser cache, so it should remain safe. However, we recommend exporting your data regularly to cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox) as an extra precaution.

Q: Can I use this on multiple devices?

A: Currently, data is stored per device. To sync across devices, export from one device and import on another.

Q: How do I restore data from a backup?

A: Go to Budget Planning → Import Data and select your exported JSON file. This will restore all your data from that point in time.

Q: What's the difference between a recurring bill and a transaction?

A: Recurring bills are expenses that happen regularly (same amount, same time each month). Transactions are one-time purchases or payments. When you log a "Bill Payment" transaction matching a bill, it counts as payment towards that bill.
